Typography

About this piece

This is a typography I made for my ARTX 2060 - Photography for non Majors class at the University of Utah. A typography in photography is the practice of taking very similar photos and placing them next to each other, in order to bring out their subtle differences. I was warned, before starting this project, that many bad photographers use typographies as an excuse for bad photos, so I decided to make sure my photos stood on their own as well as together. I found an old laser diode from some broken electronics, and I started messing around with it. I quickly found that it created a beautiful and ominous effect when lighting objects in pitch black, so I ran with that. I built a custom black box to use as a background, cut the lights, grabbed my camera, and lit up some pill bottles with lasers. I chose pill bottles because they seemed to have the best results, glowing with intensity and causing a very strange fuzzy vision effect to the naked eye. It is a very personal piece because of this; It feels as if the number of pill bottles are a measure of my health. It does not comment at all on pharmaceutical companies or western medicine, as images of pill bottles often do. It is rather a vulnerable piece for me that proves I should not be ashamed of my disabilities.
